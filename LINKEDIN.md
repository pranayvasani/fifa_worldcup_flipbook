I spent a weekend with 92 years of World Cup data and built something I wish existed.

964 matches. 2,720 goals. 22 tournaments. One interactive flipbook.

The structure is two chapters, on purpose:

📺 **The Known** — three things every pundit already says, confirmed with numbers.
💡 **The Aha** — four findings that are sitting right there in the data, that nobody talks about.

A few that surprised me:

→ The Soviet Union won 58% of their group matches and 14% of their knockouts. Statistically the best team in the world at the part of the tournament that doesn't count.

→ West Germany converted 13 of 14 shootout penalties — 92.9%. We've written a library about England's misses. Nobody asks why Germany never missed.

→ Since 2010, 10.2% of all World Cup goals happen *after* the 90th minute. Before 1990, that number was zero. The final whistle is not where you think it is.

The build itself was an experiment: can a "data essay" feel like flipping through a newspaper instead of scrolling through a dashboard? I think yes — but you tell me.
